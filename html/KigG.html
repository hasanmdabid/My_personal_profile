KIgG

AI-supported in-vitro diagnostics for autoimmune diseases

The KigG project utilizes innovative testing methods that simulate immunological processes outside the body to identify characteristic patterns. This approach enables differentiated diagnostics that address the individual disease progression of patients. "With KigG, we can effectively bridge the interface between basic research and clinical application, thus making a significant contribution to the further development of precision medicine," Dr. Hackel continued. expandAI GmbH supports the project with its expertise in machine learning and AI-based digitalization. Prof. Dr.-Ing. Marcin Grzegorzek, founder and shareholder of expandAI GmbH, and Artur Piet, CEO of the company, contribute their technical and strategic experience to the development of the diagnostic technology. The BMBF's DATIpilot funding guideline, with its "Innovation Sprints" module, offers a flexible and rapid way to translate innovative ideas into practice. Simplified application processes and short project durations of a maximum of 18 months provide researchers with an experimental space to test forward-looking technologies. The KigG project exemplifies how targeted knowledge transfer can generate concrete societal benefits from research results.

Project Details

  • Project Duration: Sept 2024 - Jan 2026
  • Funding: Federal Ministry of Education and Research (BMBF)
  • Project Partners: University Hospital Schleswig-Holstein (UKSH).
  • Project Website: KIgG.de

My Contributions

In this project, I am responsible for developing and implementing machine learning algorithms to analyze complex immunological data. My work focuses on creating predictive models that can identify disease patterns and support personalized treatment strategies for autoimmune diseases.

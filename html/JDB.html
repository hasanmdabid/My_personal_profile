JDB short-term prediction

This project developed a sales forecasting model to predict half-hourly sales turnover for various products and store locations. It aimed to improve upon the limitations of the current Random Forest (RF) model, which struggles with fine-grained predictions and sequential data. The project explores and evaluates several advanced deep learning models, including DNNs, LSTMs, GRUs, Mamba, and Hybrid models, which are better suited for handling complex time-series data.
The goal was to find a highly accurate model that could predict sales at a granular level, even with irregular sales patterns and frequent zero-turnover periods. The models were evaluated using a range of metrics (MAE, RMSE, WAPE) and different prediction approaches (direct, cumulative, and daily turnover) to ensure a comprehensive assessment. By demonstrating that deep learning models significantly outperform the existing RF method, this project provides a valuable solution for retailers to reduce costs, optimize inventory, and enhance customer satisfaction. The final report details the entire process, from data preparation and model training to evaluation and results analysis.

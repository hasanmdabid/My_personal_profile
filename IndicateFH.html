INDICATE-FH - New approaches to the diagnosis and treatment of food intolerances

More and more people are unable to tolerate wheat. The reason for this can be a wheat allergy or gluten intolerance (celiac disease), both of which are clearly defined illnesses. However, wheat intolerance often has other causes, the diagnosis of which has so far been very complex. The gastrointestinal symptoms are sometimes misdiagnosed as irritable bowel syndrome, leading to suboptimal treatment. The main goal of the "INDICATE-FH" project is to improve the diagnosis and treatment of wheat-related food intolerances. To this end, the interdisciplinary project team will develop diagnostic procedures that can distinguish the various causes of wheat intolerance and differentiate them from irritable bowel syndrome. Among other things, a special form of endoscopy – confocal laser endomicroscopy (CLE) – is being optimized. The analysis of endoscopic images is to be improved using artificial intelligence. Several subprojects are also working on diagnosing wheat intolerances based on blood, saliva, or stool samples. Another approach is further developing an app that collects data from wearables and data on diet, lifestyle, and symptoms. Based on the findings on the diagnosis of wheat intolerance, patients can be offered a more specific, less stressful nutritional therapy in the future.

Project Details

My Contributions

I was involved in the project as a Data Scientist, focusing on the development of AI-based diagnostic tools and the analysis of data collected from various sources. Food hypersensitivity (FHS) is a relatively common pathological condition characterised by adverse reactions to specific foods, and that currently has limited diagnostic methods. Diagnostic tools using gastro-intestinal confocal laser endomicroscopy (CLE) images have recently been proposed for the assessment of FHS, but their interpretation can be challenging even for trained physicians. We propose to alleviate this problem by training machine learning models on CLE images of the gastro-intestinal tract for the binary classification problem of recognising images that show an adverse reaction to food intake. My research shows that although all four models achieve satisfying classification performances, they learn very different features in terms of interpretability from the clinical perspective.

Research Outcome

  • Deep-learning-based Detection of Food Hypersensitivity from Confocal Laser Endomicroscopy Images of the Gastro-intestinal Tract
